The primary areas of concentration are mechanical analysis and design, fluids engineering, thermal sciences, robotics, manufacturing, and industrial engineering. Students may select basic and applied courses dealing with such topics as fluid mechanics, heat and mass transfer, thermodynamics, combustion, stress analysis, noise and vibrations, mechanisms, mechanical design, dynamics and control, computer aided design and manufacturing, materials processing, production engineering, and human factors.  Currently, the department offers graduate programs in: Mechanical Engineering, Industrial Engineering, and Energy Engineering .
