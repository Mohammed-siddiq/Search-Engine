                    <h2 id="david-randolph" class="__title visible">David Randolph</h2>
                    </div>

  
  <p>PhD in Computer Science | Joliet, IL</p>
<p><strong>Academic focus: </strong>Music informatics, machine learning, educational technology</p>
<p><strong>The UIC advantage: </strong>The department advertised a part-time PhD option. It was the only such program I found when I was scouting for somewhere to continue my studies. I was working full-time and needed a place that would be flexible.</p>
<p><strong>Dissertation title: </strong>Actionable Computational Models of Piano Fingering</p>
<p><strong>A snapshot of PhD life: </strong>I have been lucky enough to collaborate with a number of musicians, engineers, psychologists, and computer scientists, both inside and outside UIC, in a true multi-disciplinary extravaganza. But I think my absolute favorite part was building a Frankenstein digital piano with a micro-controller, foil tape, some mysterious electronic components, and no prior soldering experience.</p>
<p><strong>Research contribution: </strong>I like to think that a system that can dispense high-quality fingering advice could someday lead to a tutorial or decision-support system that will help pianists prepare pieces for performance. Better fingering = better music. Also, as injury is a serious issue among pianists, such a system might help pianists to strike a healthier balance between ergonomics and musical expressiveness.</p>



  
</div>
