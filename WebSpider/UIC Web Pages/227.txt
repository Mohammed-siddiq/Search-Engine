class="ptl_top_portlet" style="display:none;"></div><div id="ctl00_ContentPlaceHolder1_ctl07_divContent"><h2>Currency</h2>
<div class="entry">
<h3>ATM:</h3>
<p>Using an ATM card is a common way to withdraw  money in the U.S. ATM  machines are very common. ATM stands for  Automatic Teller Machine, and  is a machine that allows you to withdraw  cash from your bank account,  without having to be at your bank. Be  aware that most banks will  charge a fee if you use ATMs of another bank  on their machine. This can  add up quickly &ndash; so be careful! <strong> </strong></p>
<p><strong> </strong></p>
<h3>Credit Card:</h3>
<p>It is also common for people to pay for items by credit  card. Visa,  MasterCard and American Express cards are usually accepted  in most  grocery stores, retail shops and restaurants. <strong> </strong></p>
<p><strong> </strong></p>
<h3>Exchanging Currency:&nbsp;</h3>
<p>Most money can be exchanged at major banks and at most <a href="http://www.mycurrencyexchange.com/">Currency Exchange Stores</a>. In addition to exchanging currency, this store provides other  services,  such as sending money through Western Union, and providing&nbsp;<a href="http://www.transitchicago.com/">CTA passes</a> for purchase.</p>
<p>The following information may be helpful to those unfamiliar with   United States currency: One dollar ($1.00) equals &ldquo;100&rdquo; cents. Paper   money or &ldquo;bills&rdquo;, come in denominations of $1, $5, $10, $20, $50, and   $100. Coins are in denominations as follows:</p>

<h2>Banking</h2>
<div class="entry">
<p><strong></strong></p>
<span id="ctl00_ContentPlaceHolder1_ctl10_ltContent">
<div class="entry">
<p><strong>The two banks which are accustomed to working with Internationals and that you can find on-campus are </strong><a href="http://www.creditunion1.org/"><strong>Credit Union 1</strong></a><strong> and </strong><strong><a href="https://www.pnc.com/en/personal-banking.html">PNC Bank</a></strong><strong>. There are also many banks around Chicago including </strong><a href="http://www.citibank.com/"><strong>Citibank</strong></a><strong>, </strong><a href="http://www.chase.com/"><strong>Chase Bank</strong></a><strong>, and </strong><a href="http://www.bankofamerica.com/"><strong>Bank of America</strong></a><strong>, to name a few. </strong></p>
<p>Following are UIC&rsquo;s on-campus resources:</p>
<p><strong>Credit Union 1</strong></p>
<p>Complete banking services are offered by <a href="http://www.creditunion1.org/">Credit Union 1</a>, which has three convenient locations on campus<strong><em>.&nbsp;</em></strong>Credit
Union 1 members can get free checking accounts and saving accounts
with  a minimum balance of $5,&nbsp;ATM/Debit cards, money orders, and
traveler&rsquo;s  checks. Clients must show a photo identification (I.D.) to
qualify for  opening an account.</p>
<p><strong>PNC Bank</strong></p>
<p><a href="https://www.pnc.com/en/personal-banking.html">PNC Bank</a>
is the banking partner   of UIC. PNC Bank offers the Student Checking
Account with online banking, bill pay, and mobile banking. PNC also
offers international wire transfers. To open a PNC Bank account, visit
the <a href="https://apps.pnc.com/locator/#/search">nearest branch</a> with your valid passport and UIC ID card.  </p>
