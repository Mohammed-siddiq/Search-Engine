        <h3><a href="http://www.cmhsrp.uic.edu/health/">UIC Center on Psychiatric Disability & Co-Occurring Conditions</a></h3>
        <p>Explore our five-year research and training program to improve well-being and quality of life among people with psychiatric disability and co-occurring medical conditions such as diabetes, heart disease, and obesity. We have pod casts, curricula, decision trees, and other new products that you can use to reduce health disparities. <a href="http://www.cmhsrp.uic.edu/health/">Visit the web site for the new Center</a></p></div>
      <div class="innerBoxSubsequent">
        <h3>SELF-DETERMINATION TOOLS</h3>
        <p>The Center provides a series of tools to advance self-determination and person-driven services for use by people with psychiatric disabilities.</p>
        <p><a href="../nrtc/tools.asp" style="background-color:#06a;color:#fff;border:1px solid #222;padding:3px 5px;font:86% arial bold;">View Tools</a></p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <p><strong>The National Research and Training Center on Psychiatric Disability (NRTC) promotes access to effective consumer-driven and community-based services for adults with serious mental illness.  The Center is located at the University of Illinois at Chicago in the Department of Psychiatry.</strong></p>
    <p>To enhance use of evidence-based practice in mental health and rehabilitation,
      a program of rigorous research examines the outcomes of recovery self-management
      models, consumer-to-consumer education, self-directed healthcare financing
      mechanisms, Medicaid-funded peer services, consumer-operated programs,
      and return-to-work services.</p>
    <p>The Center provides data-driven information, technical assistance, and
      training that foster evidence translation, systems transformation, and
      workforce development. Training and education include projects on consumer
      leadership development, client self-advocacy, peer provider certification,
      financial asset development, preparing for research careers, and inpatient
      seclusion and restraint reduction.</p>
    <p>An innovative academic curricular transformation effort incorporates principles
      of recovery and evidence-based practice into university instruction in
      the medical, social, and behavioral sciences. Also offered are online workshops,
      web casts, web-based continuing education courses, and a state of the science <a href="summit2009/summit-home.asp">national summit</a> held in  April 2009.</p>
