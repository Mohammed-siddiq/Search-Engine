<div class="trigger field field-name-field-accordion-title field-type-text field-label-hidden"><h3 >Do I need a bachelor&#039;s degree to be admitted into graduate school?</h3></div><div class="target field field-name-field-accordion-body field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><p>Completion of a baccalaureate degree, or an acceptable equivalent, from an accredited institution is required for Graduate level study; some programs may also require a professional school degree, master’s degree, or certification. Foreign degrees are evaluated by Admissions staff to determine equivalency.</p>

<div class="trigger field field-name-field-accordion-title field-type-text field-label-hidden"><h3 >Which citizenship category do I fall under? How do I know if I am categorized as an international applicant?</h3></div><div class="target field field-name-field-accordion-body field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><p>Regardless of your current residence, you are considered to be an international applicant if you currently hold any type of visa or would need a visa to study in the US. The graduate application determines the appropriate status based on the information provided. Here is a brief description of some of the citizenship categories:</p>
<ul><li><strong>US Citizen - </strong>Born or naturalized.</li>
<li><strong>U.S. Permanent Resident -</strong> Green card holder. You may be asked to submit a copy of your PR card within the application.</li>
<li><strong>International -</strong> Current holders of any type of visa or those who would need a visa to study in the US.</li>
<li><strong>Adjustment in Status - </strong>This category is for applicants who are in the process of becoming a permanent resident. You should submit any documentation that can provide details of your status such as receipts or petitions from the USCIS.</li>
<li><strong>Non-citizen (Other) - </strong>This should only be used if none of the other categories apply to you. Valid visa holders or those who would need a visa to study in the US should not select this category.</li>
</ul></div><!-- /Template file: sites/all/themes/custom/uicrec/templates/field-collection/ds-reset- -field-collection-item-field-accordion-item-unlimited.tpl.php -->

<div class="trigger field field-name-field-accordion-title field-type-text field-label-hidden"><h3 >I&#039;m a legal permanent resident. Do I follow domestic or international guidelines?</h3></div><div class="target field field-name-field-accordion-body field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><p>Legal permanent residents are oftentimes caught between policies for domestic and international students.</p>
<p>We encourage legal permanent residents to apply early since foreign credentials may take some time for our office to evaluate and in some cases additional documentation would need to be obtained from your previous institution. Legal permanent residents, however, are considered domestic applicants and may follow domestic deadlines.</p>
<p>Permanent residents or others with foreign coursework should also:</p>
<ul><li>Review the academic credential and qualification requirements noted for the country in which you studied.</li>
<li>Review the English proficiency requirements. A waiver is not granted based on residency in the U.S. alone. </li>
</ul></div><!-- /Template file: sites/all/themes/custom/uicrec/templates/field-collection/ds-reset- -field-collection-item-field-accordion-item-unlimited.tpl.php -->

<p>If you are a current applicant, please <a href="/graduate-professional/contact-graduate-and-professional-admissions">Contact Us</a> to notify us of changes to your contact information.</p>

<div class="trigger field field-name-field-accordion-title field-type-text field-label-hidden"><h3 >What are the minimum GPA and test score requirements? What if my GPA or scores fall below the minimums?</h3></div><div class="target field field-name-field-accordion-body field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><p>The Graduate College requires an undergraduate grade point average of at least 2.75 (A= 4.00) for the final 60 semester (90 quarter) hours of baccalaureate study (typically around two years), and all post-baccalaureate coursework.</p>
<p>English proficiency minimums are noted on our International Requirements page.</p>
<p>Programs typically do not set minimum scores for the GRE and other tests that measure academic aptitude. You may follow up with the program directly to inquire if they can provide you with a score range they would consider ideal for consideration of admission.</p>
<p>If GPA or English proficiency scores fall below the minimums, it does not mean that an application will be automatically denied. The application will still be considered. Other aspects of the application may lead the program to determine that they would like to recommend admission.</p>
<p>In some cases, additional approval from the Graduate College would be needed. Also, the program may recommend admission with Limited Status, meaning that an applicant would have certain grade or course requirements during their first term or year.</p>
</div></div>

<div class="trigger field field-name-field-accordion-title field-type-text field-label-hidden"><h3 >I&#039;m currently enrolled in a Graduate degree program at UIC. Do I need to apply to be admitted to another Graduate degree program?</h3></div><div class="target field field-name-field-accordion-body field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><p>Currently enrolled degree-seeking students do not need to submit an application in order to change or add a program. Instead, a Request for Change of Graduate Program should be completed. Students wishing to change or add a program should refer to the <a href="http://grad.uic.edu/change-graduate-program">Change of Graduate Program page</a> and contact their current department for further information. This would include cases such as:</p>
<ul><li>Completing the Master's degree during the Spring term and would like to pursue admission the PhD program for the subsequent Fall term.</li>
<li>Currently enrolled in one graduate degree program, but would like to switch to a different graduate degree program, perhaps in a different department or college.</li>
</ul><p>Graduate degree-seeking applications submitted for currently enrolled Graduate degree-seeking students may be withdrawn by our office, as they are not needed.</p>

  <div class="col-sm-8 ">
	  <div class="content field field-name-field-accordion-item-unlimited field-type-field-collection field-label-hidden" ><div class="odd" ><!-- Template file: sites/all/themes/custom/uicrec/templates/field-collection/ds-reset- -field-collection-item-field-accordion-item-unlimited.tpl.php -->
<div class="trigger field field-name-field-accordion-title field-type-text field-label-hidden"><h3 >I am trying to apply online but the program or term isn’t in the drop-down menu. What should I do?</h3></div><div class="target field field-name-field-accordion-body field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><p>There are a few possible reasons why your intended program or desired term may not be available on the Intended Program page within the application:</p>
<ul><li>If a program is not available:
<ul><li>You may have selected the wrong application type within the application (i.e. Doctorate, Master, Non-degree, etc.)</li>
<li>The program may not have an open application at the time. A few programs open later than others for a shorter window of time.</li>
<li>The program may not be offered at UIC. See the <a href="https://catalog.uic.edu/gcat/degree-programs/grad-prof-degree-programs/" target="_blank">Graduate Catalog</a> for a list of programs offered.</li>
</ul></li>
<li>If a term is not available:
<ul><li>The program may not have an open application at the time. A few programs open later than others for a shorter window of time.</li>
</ul></li>
<li>If a term is available but noted as past deadline:
<ul><li>The deadline has passed for the selected program and term. Note that for many programs application submission deadlines for international students are earlier than the domestic applicant deadline. </li>
<li>Check the <a href="/graduate-professional/deadlines">Application Deadlines</a> page to see whether your program is still accepting applications. All programs have a deadline for each term – even non-degree applications.</li>
</ul></li>
<li><strong>You may not have accessed the correct application option or link.</strong> Verify that you’re accessing the correct link and Application Type for your intended program. Some applications may only be accessed from the program's site.</li>
</ul><p>If your intended program or term is not available within the application, please <strong>do not</strong> submit the application with another program or term in order to complete the application. Doing so will result in delays and may inhibit departmental access to your records, or the application may be canceled.</p>
<p><a href="/graduate-professional/contact-graduate-and-professional-admissions">Contact us</a> and we’ll point you in the right direction.</p>

<div class="trigger field field-name-field-accordion-title field-type-text field-label-hidden"><h3 >When can I upload my academic credentials and my supplemental program requirements?</h3></div><div class="target field field-name-field-accordion-body field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><p>Application materials should be uploaded within the application.</p>
<ul><li>Upload slots for credentials become available based upon the the degrees reported in your academic history.</li>
<li>Upload slots for supplemental documents are available based upon the program requirements.</li>
<li>If letters of recommendation are required for the program, applicants are prompted to provide email addresses of recommenders. The application system will then send the recommender a request to submit the recommendation.</li>
</ul><p>Applicants are able to submit the application before uploading all supplemental program requirements. They can then be uploaded after submission via the application status page. Applications would remain in an incomplete status if all requirements are not received.</p>
