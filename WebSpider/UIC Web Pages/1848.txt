<h2><a name="Associate_Professor_Jakob_Erikss"></a> Associate Professor Jakob Eriksson </h2>
<p />
I grew up in Sweden, got my M.Sc. in CS at the <a href="http://www.nada.kth.se">Royal Institute of Technology</a> in <a href="http://www.google.com/images?q=stockholm">Stockholm</a>. Wasted a few years on dot-coms, before graduating with a Ph.D. from <a href="http://www.cs.ucr.edu">U.C. Riverside</a> in '06. I then spent a two-year postdoc at <a href="http://nms.csail.mit.edu">MIT</a>.  I've been a faculty member of the UIC Department of Computer Science since 2009, and was promoted to Associate Professor in 2015.
<p />
My wife <a href="http://www.ece.uic.edu/Devroye" target="_top">Prof. Devroye</a> is in the ECE department. We live in the near-west suburb of Oak Park together with our two children Matilda and Oskar. 
<p />
<h2><a name="Research_Interests"></a> Research Interests </h2>
<p />
My research interests lie primarily in computer systems, including operating systems, networks and mobile sensing. In operating systems, I work on deterministic execution, high-performance messaging systems, and alternative uses for simultaneous multi-threading. In the mobile and networked systems area, some of my work includes vehicular WiFi connectivity, and mobile sensing for traffic monitoring and road condition assessment. You can read more about my research projects on my <a href="/bin/view/Bits/WebHome" class="twikiLink">research lab's web</a>.
