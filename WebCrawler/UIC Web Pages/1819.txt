Professor and Department Head
<p />
<h1><a name="Contact"></a> Contact </h1>
<p />
Robert Sloan<br /> Dept. of Computer Science (M/C 152) <br /> University of Illinois at Chicago <br /> 851 S. Morgan Street (Room 1120) <br /> Chicago, IL 60607-7053 <br /> Phone: 312-996-2369 <br />Email: my-last-name at host: uic.edu.
<p />
<h1><a name="What_s_Important_to_Me"></a> What's Important to Me </h1> <ul>
<li> I am a proud <a href="https://braidresearch.gseis.ucla.edu/" target="_top">BRAID</a> Department Head.
</li> <li> <em>You </em>should study Computer Science, and I'm writing about it. Download and read Robert H. Sloan, <a href="https://www.cs.uic.edu/pub/Sloan/WebHome/SloanWhy_Computer_Science_To_Appear.pdf" target="_top">"Why Computer Science?"</a> (in a collection, written just before enrollments went from high to extraordinarily high).
</li> <li> Richard Warner's and my book: <em> <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Unauthorized-Access-Crisis-Privacy-Security/dp/1439830134" target="_top">Unauthorized Access: The Crisis in Online Privacy and Security</a>, </em>by Robert H. Sloan and <a href="http://www.kentlaw.iit.edu/faculty/full-time-faculty/richard-warner" target="_blank">Richard Warner</a>, CRC Press, 2013.
</li> <li> Now under contract: A mini-book followup, out late 2018 or early 2019.
</li></ul>

<h2><a name="Computer_Science_Education_Model"></a> Computer Science Education: Model curricula, etc. </h2>
<p />
I was a coauthor of (i.e., member of the relevant joint ACM and IEEE-Computer Society task force for): <ul>
<li> <em>Computing Curricula 2001: Computer Science</em>.
</li> <li> <em>Computing Curricula 2004: Computer Engineering</em>.
</li> <li> <em>Computing Curricula 2005: The Overview Report</em>.
</li> <li> <em>CS2008 Curriculum Update. </em>(2008 light revision of <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-style: italic;">Computing Curricula 2001: Computer Science</span>)
</li></ul> 
All those reports are available <a href="http://www.acm.org/education/education/curricula-recommendations" target="_top">here</a>.
<p />
<h1><a name="Research"></a> Research </h1>
<p />
Research interests:
<p /> <ol>
<li> Computer security, especially privacy, legal, and policy issues.
</li> <li> Application of computer science theory and algorithms to problems from artificial intelligence. Proud member of the <a href="http://theory.cs.uic.edu" target="_top">UIC theory group</a>. Currently especially focused on problems from knowledge representation. Previously especially focused on problems from machine learning ("computational learning theory").
</li> <li> Computer Science Education.
</li></ol>
