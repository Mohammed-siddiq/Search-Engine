  <p><span style="font-size: x-large;"><strong>The Engineering Career Center is dedicated to supporting UIC international students with their job and/or internship search.     <div class="component-description">
                    <h2 id="cpt" class="__title invisible">cpt</h2>
                    </div>

  
  <ul>
<li>All international students interning or conducting research off-campus must enroll in either <strong>ENGR 289</strong> or <strong>598 (Master’s research)</strong> or <strong>599 (PhD research)</strong> course in their major.</li>
<li><em><strong>Before meeting with the ECC,</strong></em> determine if you need to register for either ENGR 289, 598, or 599.</li>
<li><strong>ENGR 289</strong> – this course is used for students who are working in an internship in order to build their professional skills and knowledge.
<ul>
<li><strong>Students must be actively taking Engineering courses at UIC in order to qualify to register for ENGR 289.</strong></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>598 or 599</strong> – these courses are used for students who want to work in an internship in order to conduct research for their thesis.
<ul>
<li>If you are <strong>NOT taking Engineering courses</strong> at UIC, then you should enroll in a 598 or 599 course to fulfill the requirements for your CPT application.</li>
</ul>

                    <h2 id="probation" class="__title invisible">Probation</h2>
                    </div>

  
  <p>Undergraduate and graduate students who are on academic probation may not apply for CPT during the academic year. Academic probation is a serious concern and puts students at risk for dismissal from UIC. Therefore, students applying for CPT during the Fall or Spring semesters will be denied &#8211; students should use their time during the academic year to focus on coursework and getting off of academic probation.</p>
<p>Undergraduate and graduate students who are on academic probation and obtain an internship over the summer months, may apply for CPT.</p>
<p>An undergraduate student is placed on academic probation at the end of any term in which the student earns less than a 2.00/4.00 grade point average. This rule applies even for the student with a cumulative GPA of 2.00/4.00 or higher. For more information on UIC&#8217;s probation and dismissal rules, <a href="https://catalog.uic.edu/ucat/degree-programs/academic-standing/#probationdismissalrules">please review the UIC course catalog</a>.</p>
<p>A graduate student is placed on academic probation when their grade point average falls below a 3.00. For more information on UIC&#8217;s probation and dismissal rules for graduate students, <a href="https://grad.uic.edu/academic-standingcontinuation-and-probation-rules">please visit the UIC Graduate College</a>.</p>
