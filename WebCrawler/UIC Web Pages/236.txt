<H2><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">ADVIS Research Laboratory</FONT></H2>
<P><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">The </FONT><B><FONT COLOR="#CC0000" FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Advances in Data, Visual, and Information Science Research Laboratory (ADVIS)</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> is part of the </FONT><A HREF="http://www.cs.uic.edu/"><FONT
FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Department of Computer Science</FONT></A><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">, </FONT><A HREF="http://www.uic.edu/depts/enga/"><FONT
FACE="Arial, Helvetica">College of Engineering</FONT></A><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> at the </FONT><A HREF="http://www.uic.edu"><FONT
FACE="Arial, Helvetica">University of Illinois at Chicago</FONT></A><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">. ADVIS hosts
students doing research in computer information management systems with particular emphasis on </FONT><B><FONT
FACE="Arial, Helvetica">database</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> and </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">information
systems</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> and related issues such as: </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">semantic
web </FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">(and in particular </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">semantic
data integration</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> and </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">semantic
desktop</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">), </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">geospatial information
systems, context-aware systems, information visualization</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> (including </FONT><B><FONT
FACE="Arial, Helvetica">graph drawing</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">), </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">retrieval
and presentation of multimedia information</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">, and </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">information
retrieval</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">and classification</FONT></B><FONT
FACE="Arial, Helvetica">.</FONT></P>
<P><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">This research lab has existed since 1994 (first with the name </FONT><B><FONT
FACE="Arial, Helvetica">DBVIS</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">, for </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">D</FONT></B><FONT
FACE="Arial, Helvetica">ata</FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">b</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">ase
</FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Vis</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">ualization) and after 1997
with the current name, reflecting the fact that we focus on all types of information and not only on information
that can be stored in and retrieved from traditional database systems. The group has comprised several PhD, MS,
and BS students collaborating in research and publishing papers in competitive international conferences and in
journals. Over the years, the group has regularly sponsored the visit of several leading US and European researchers
and of postdocs.<BR>
</FONT><BR>

<P><A NAME="Projects"></A><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Projects</FONT></B></P>
<P><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">The group is currently working on six projects:</FONT>

<UL>
	<LI><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Data Interoperability for Geospatial Applications</FONT><SUP><FONT COLOR="white">T</FONT></SUP></B><FONT
	FACE="Arial, Helvetica">This project integrates and extends Computer Science research in the Database Management
	System (DBMS) area with a State of Wisconsin Land Information System (WLIS). Web-based DBMS user interface, search,
	and query techniques will be incorporated in WLIS. The concept of WLIS as a statewide access mechanism for land
	related data in Wisconsin has been actively under development for several years by a working group that includes
	various levels of government and the University of Wisconsin. WLIS will be a distributed Web-based system with
	heterogeneous data residing on local and state servers. A large part of WLIS is spatial, Geographic Information
	Systems (GIS) data. Collaborating institutions: UIC (lead) and University of Wisconsin-Madison. (</FONT><A HREF="http://www.cs.uic.edu/~ifc/webpapers/cruz-xiao-revised.pdf"><FONT
	FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Paper</FONT></A><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">)</FONT>
	<LI><B><A HREF="http://www.cs.uic.edu/~advis/CASSIS"><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">CASSIS: Context-Aware Computing
	with Applications to Public Health Management</FONT></A></B> <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">This project creates
	an architecture for a system that provides comprehensive support for context-aware applications. More than 130
	</FONT><A HREF="http://www.cs.uic.edu/~advis/CASSIS/publications.html"><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">publications</FONT></A><FONT
	FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> have resulted from this project among collaborating institutions UIC (lead), Brown University
	and Northwestern University. The current research emphasis by the ADVIS group is on an extension to a role-based
	access control (RBAC) approach to provide for the dynamic association of roles with users in collaborative environments.
	(</FONT><A HREF="http://www.cs.uic.edu/~ifc/webpapers/CollCom-ready-3.pdf"><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Paper</FONT></A><FONT
	FACE="Arial, Helvetica">)</FONT>
	<LI><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Semantic Desktop</FONT></B> <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">With the Semantic
	Web vision, a number of Personal Information Management Systems have been proposed [</FONT><A HREF="http://www.semanticdesktop.org/"><FONT
	FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Web page for the 1st Workshop on the Semantic Desktop</FONT></A><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">].
	In our approach, we propose: (1) Semantic data organization based on multiple layers of ontologies; (2) Flexible
	data manipulation provided by data interoperation and supported by an expressive query language; and (3) Rich visual
	manipulation that uses the underlying semantic connections among data. (</FONT><A HREF="http://www.cs.uic.edu/~ifc/webpapers/cruz-xiao-vldbj-08.pdf"><FONT
	FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Paper</FONT></A><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">)</FONT>
	<LI><B><A HREF="http://www.cs.uic.edu/~ifc/grants/SchemaOntologyMatching/index.html"><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Efficient
	Matching for Large Real-World Schemas and Ontologies</FONT></A></B> <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">This project
	extends the state of the art in schema and ontology matching, and therefore in data integration, by testing and
	evaluating methods and strategies that establish relationships among semantically related concepts in heterogeneous
	data sources.<BR>
	The following research issues are addressed: (1) Design of methods and algorithms for schema and ontology matching
	that operate at different levels of granularity (e.g., concept, structure); (2) Development of a prototype of an
	integrated system that supports the visualization and manipulation of large schemas and ontologies in addition
	to the developed matching methods and algorithms; (3) Test and evaluation of the above methods and system prototype
	in terms of their effectiveness, including accuracy (precision, recall) and efficiency (execution time). (</FONT><A
	HREF="http://www.cs.uic.edu/~ifc/webpapers/gis_trans-publish.pdf"><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Paper</FONT></A><FONT
	FACE="Arial, Helvetica">)</FONT>
	<LI><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Privacy-preserving record and image mapping</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">
	In this project we consider methods to perform record and image mapping in a privacy preserving way, more specifically,
	we will develop mapping procedures with privacy guarantees for the parties involved in the mapping process. Quality
	measures will be developed and uncertainty will be considered. Collaborating institutions: UIC (lead), Brown University,
	Rutgers University, University of Rome &quot;La Sapienza.&quot; (Preliminary </FONT><A HREF="http://www.cs.uic.edu/~ifc/webpapers/poster.pdf"><FONT
	FACE="Arial, Helvetica">paper</FONT></A><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">)</FONT>
	<LI><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Query Languages for Heterogeneous Transportation Networks</FONT></B><FONT
	FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> We are exploring textual and visual query languages for graphs that represent urban transportation networks.
	Issues to explore include the syntax and semantics of the languages, their expressive power and query evaluation.
	This project is part of an </FONT><A HREF="http://cts.cs.uic.edu/"><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">IGERT grant</FONT></A><FONT
	FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> at UIC. (</FONT><A HREF="http://www.cs.uic.edu/~ifc/webpapers/edbt2009-submission.pdf"><FONT
	FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Paper</FONT></A><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">)</FONT>
</UL>

<P><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Other projects of the group:</FONT></P>

<UL>
	<LI><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Visualization and Integration of Heterogeneous Information</FONT></B><FONT
	FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> (</FONT><B><FONT COLOR="#CC0000" FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Delaunay</FONT></B><SUP><B><FONT
	COLOR="#CC0000" FACE="Arial, Helvetica">MM</FONT></B></SUP><B><FONT COLOR="#CC0000" FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> and
	Delaunay</FONT></B><SUP><B><FONT COLOR="#CC0000" FACE="Arial, Helvetica">View</FONT></B></SUP><B><FONT COLOR="#CC0000"
	FACE="Arial, Helvetica">systems</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">)</FONT>
	<P><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">This system tightly integrates a visual query language for distributed queries
	with the layout specification that can emphasize the information semantics. We are continuing this work in several
	directions, including the use of ontologies to aid in query refinement, the integration of security concerns, and
	the creation of metadata for the synchronized retrieval and presentation of the information.</FONT></P>
	
	<LI><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Visual Query Languages</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> (</FONT><B><FONT
	COLOR="#CC0000" FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Delaunay system</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">)</FONT><B><SUP><FONT
	COLOR="white">M</FONT></SUP></B>
	<P><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">The basis of this system is a visual meta-query language for displaying and querying
	object-oriented databases, called DOODLE. Features of the system include the support of user defined query languages
	and visualizations. Currently we are continuing work on: (1) semi-automatic visualizations; (2) use of metadata
	to generate visualizations; and (3) using dynamic constraint solving for positioning optional visual components.</FONT><BR>
	<BR>
	
	<LI><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">PANDA</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> (Publishing Assistant for Networked
	Document Assembly)</FONT>
	<P><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">This project is on a powerful authoring tool that integrates and presents marked-up
	information that is available on the web and gives powerful customization capabilities to the users (publishers
	and end-users). The result of this research is being directly incorporated into the ACM SIGMOD Digital Symposium
	Collection. We are extending PANDA to incorporate: visual query languages and metadata support.<BR>
	<BR>
	</FONT>
	<LI><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">CASA</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> (Classification using a Structural
	Approach)</FONT>
	<P><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">We have been investigating automatic classification of Web documents. We studied
	the recall based on the structural analysis of more than 600,000 Web documents. We are investigating new algorithms
	that improve recall.<BR>
	<BR>
	</FONT>
	<LI><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">MATIC</FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"> (Multimedia Authoring with
	Time Constraints)</FONT>
	<P><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">This is a multimedia authoring project for the temporal synchronization of multimedia
	streams. We have devised an efficient algorithm that determines whether synchronization can be achieved. Future
	work includes integrating this approach into SMIL.<BR>
	<BR>
	</FONT>
	<LI><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Mocha</FONT></B>
	<P><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">This project optimally partitions the software components of a typical algorithm
	animation system and leverages the power of the Java language. This system is currently used for geometric algorithms
	(with the Computational Geometry Center at Brown U.).<BR>
	<BR>
	</FONT>
	<LI><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">WebSA </FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">(Web Site Agent)</FONT>
	<P><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">In this project users upon entering a Web site are given hints reflecting the
	interests of the previous thousands of visits to the site. In order to take into account the information previously
	gathered, efficient database mining of the previously collected data and the building of relevant aggregate information
	are performed. We are developing other collaborative filtering algorithms within the same architecture.<BR>
	<BR>
	</FONT></P>
	<LI><B><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">ACM DiSC</FONT></B>
	<P><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">Until 2001 the lab was &quot;home&quot; to the </FONT><A HREF="http://www.acm.org/sigs/sigmod/disc/disc01/index.htm"><FONT
	FACE="Arial, Helvetica">ACM SIGMOD Digital Symposium Collection (DiSC)</FONT></A><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica">,
	an electronic journal that is the most extensive yearly source of database publications.</FONT><BR>
	
</UL>
