<div  about="/field-collection/field-mult-content/638" typeof="" id="decisions" class="ds-2col row entity entity-field-collection-item field-collection-item-field-mult-content view-mode-full clearfix anchor section ">

  <div class="col-sm-4 ">
    <h2 >Admission Decisions</h2>  </div>
  
  <div class="col-sm-8 ">
	  <div class="content field field-name-field-section-body field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><h3>If you're not sure what your admission decision means, please see below. </h3>
<h4><strong>Admit</strong></h4>
<p>A decision of Admit is a full standing admission. </p>
<h4>Conditional Admit</h4>
<p>Conditional admission means that you were admitted with full standing, but still need to submit final, official academic credentials since the admission decision was made based upon unofficial credentials or while your prior degree program was still in progress. The application checklist and university admission letter will both indicate the official credentials that you will need to submit.</p>
<p>Conditionally admitted students <strong>can enroll and study during their first term at UIC, but will not be able to register for their second term until the requested official credentials are received</strong>. A registration hold is placed on the student record which takes effect after the registration period of the first term. We recommend that all students clear their pending conditions by submitting the required credentials after being admitted, when they arrive on campus, or shortly therafter. Admissions holds will not be released until the required documentation is submitted to the Office of Admissions.</p>
<p>International students: Please see the guidelines for official credentials at the bottom of the <a href="/graduate-professional/requirements-deadlines/international-requirements">International Requirements page</a>. Please also note that conditional admission does not impact visa processing by the Office of International Services.</p>
<h4>Admit - Limited Status</h4>
<p>An admission with limited status means that you were admitted to the university with the approval of the program (and Graduate College) in a probationary status due to certain deficiencies in your application (i.e., low GPA, missing pre-requisite coursework, etc.).</p>
<p>Students admitted with limited status must meet certain criteria within their first term or year in order to remain in the program. The specific details concerning the conditions of limited status admission are communicated to students by the program directly.</p>
<h4>Conditional Admit - Limited Status</h4>
<p>Conditionally admitted students with limited status are those that have been admitted with limited status and must also submit final, official credentials. Please see the two previous sections for details. </p>
<h4>Admit - Clear</h4>
<p>Conditionally admitted students will receive an Admit - Clear decision after acceptable final, official credentials are received. An email notification is sent to confirm that the pending credential requirements have been satisfied. The hold placed by the Office of Admissions will also be removed at this time.</p>
<p>The Admit - Clear decision status pertains only to credential requirements for the Office of Admissions and does not pertain to limited status conditions.</p>
</div>  </div>

</div>

  <div class="col-sm-4 ">
    <h2 >Deferring Admission</h2>  </div>
  
  <div class="col-sm-8 ">
	  <div class="content field field-name-field-section-body field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><h3>Admitted graduate and professional degree-seeking students may <strong>defer admission.</strong></h3>
<p>Admission can be deferred <strong>only once for up to one academic year</strong> for students unable to enroll in their initial term of admission. Deferral requests are subject to approval by the program and Office of Admissions. Non-degree admission cannot be deferred.</p>
<p><strong>Start by notifying your program</strong> that you would like to defer. Programs must approve deferrals first. They will then forward the request to the Office of Admissions for final approval. If approved, a new admission record will be created for the deferral term and a new admission letter will be sent out.</p>
<p>International students in need of a visa who would like to defer that have already submitted financial documents should also contact the Office of International Services to determine if new financial documents will need to be submitted.</p>
</div>  </div>

</div>

    <h2 >Additional Resources</h2>  </div>
  
  <div class="col-sm-8 ">
	  <div class="content field field-name-field-section-body field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><h3>Financial aid</h3>
<p><strong>To be considered for U.S. Federal Aid</strong>, complete the Free Application for <a href="https://studentaid.ed.gov/sa/fafsa" target="_blank">Federal Student Aid (FAFSA)</a>. Please note the FAFSA priority deadline is March 1st. International and Nondegree and campus certificate students do not qualify for Federal Aid.</p>
<p><strong>Assistantships and other departmental aid</strong> is determined by the departments. Contact the department directly for inquiries about departmental aid<strong>.</strong><br />
 </p>
<h3>Campus housing</h3>
<p>If you would like to live on campus, it is important to apply early. Please use the <a href="https://housing.uic.edu/apply/" target="_blank">online campus housing application</a>.</p>
<p>Housing options for Graduate students are available.</p>
<h3>Medical immunization forms</h3>
<p>Students planning to live in on-campus housing will need to complete the <a href="https://registrar.uic.edu/forms/medical-immunization-form.pdf" target="_blank">On-Campus Housing Medical Immunization Form</a>.</p>

  <div class="col-sm-4 ">
    <h2 >Resolving Student Holds</h2>  </div>
  
  <div class="col-sm-8 ">
	  <div class="content field field-name-field-section-body field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><h3>Holds impacting registration may be placed on the records of new students for various reasons. </h3>
<p>We've compiled the information below to help newly admitted and continuing students determine who to contact for help. Unfortunately, the Office of Admissions cannot resolve all holds that a student may have. Students can see holds affecting them through Student Self-Service, in the Student Records and Transcripts section, under the Registration tab.</p>
<p>Holds affecting registration for a student's first term at UIC:</p>
<ul><li><strong>International students</strong> have a <strong>SEVIS hold </strong>placed on their record at the time of admission. Check in with the <strong>Office of International Services</strong> when you arrive on campus to clear this hold. </li>
<li>Most departments place an <strong>Advising hold</strong> <strong>on newly admitted students</strong>. Please follow up with your <strong>program </strong>directly to clear this hold. </li>
<li><strong>UIC undergraduates who have submitted an Intent to Graduate</strong> may have a <strong>Pending Graduation Registration hold</strong> placed on their record by the Office of the Registrar so that they don't continue to register under their undergraduate program after obtaining the Bachelor's degree. Please follow up with the <strong>Office of the Registrar</strong> to clear this hold.</li>
</ul><p>Holds affecting registration for a student's second term and later:</p>
<ul><li><strong>Conditionally admitted students</strong> have an <strong>Admissions hold</strong> placed on their records which takes effect <strong><em>after</em></strong> the registration period for the student's <em><strong>first</strong></em> term at UIC. Submit the requested final/official credentials to the <strong>Office of Admissions</strong> to clear the hold.</li>
<li>An <strong>Immunizations hold</strong> may be placed on a student's record if they live in On-Campus housing and required Immunizations forms have not been received.</li>
</ul><p>Holds placed by <strong>UIUC</strong> and <strong>UIS</strong> may also affect students at UIC.</p>
<p>If you're not sure how to get a hold resolved, please <a href="https://admissions.uic.edu/graduate-professional/contact-graduate-and-professional-admissions" target="_blank">Contact Us</a> and we'll point you in the right direction.</p>
</div>  </div>
