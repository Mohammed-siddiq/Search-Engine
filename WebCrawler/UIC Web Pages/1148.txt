                    <h2 id="how-does-this-work" class="__title visible">How does this work?</h2>
                    </div>

  
  <p>You request a site through our <a href="http://red.uic.edu/contact/">Contact Us form</a>. We give you web space on the multisite, which is hosted on servers managed by ACCC. Assistance with training and ongoing multisite support is provided by Digital Communications.</p>
<p>Your website’s url will be <em>yoursite</em>.red.uic.edu (we will help you remove the .red part when you&#8217;re ready to go public). You’ll get a login link and an Administrator account, which will allow you to configure and update your site as long as you sign in with your UIC <a href="http://accc.uic.edu/tag/netid">netID</a>. If you want to collaborate, you can allow others to edit your site if they have netIDs. As the site admin, you can control how much they can do by giving them roles such as “Editor” or “Writer.”</p>
<p>Please note that your initial red.uic.edu site will be in private mode. While your site is in private mode, only you and people with accounts on your site will be able to see it.</p>
<p>When your site is built out and you’re ready to launch, fill out our site request form again. We will review the site for any accessibility or usability issues and work with you to address any we find before we make your site public and remove the “red” part from the URL.</p>
